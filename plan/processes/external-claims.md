# Process — External claims & contributed methods

**Discipline:** Evidence & evaluation · **Room:** the laboratory · **Owning agent:** Referee ·
**Skill:** `evaluate-external-claim`

> Someone brings a method. Harvest the testable core, refuse the metaphysics, and answer
> with kindness and a protocol — never with endorsement or dismissal.

## Trigger
Any outside party offers the Foundation a method, framework, prompt pattern, protocol, or
finding — especially one arriving with strong claims and weak measurement.

## The governing stance
A governance foundation cannot endorse by narrative. But it also must not dismiss sincere
practitioners — most arrive with a real observation wrapped in borrowed vocabulary. So the
answer is always the same shape: **name the small testable core, exclude the rest
explicitly, and reply with specific, falsifiable questions.** The claimant gets respect and
a protocol; the field gets evidence; the Foundation risks nothing it can't defend to an
auditor.

## Repeatable steps  *(the agent does the bureaucracy)*
1. **Split the artifact in two.** List (a) what is concrete, bounded, and observable, and
   (b) what is metaphor, borrowed physics, or unfalsifiable framing. Write both lists
   plainly — the exclusion list matters as much as the harvest.
2. **Restate the harvest in house language.** Translate each keepable idea into the
   estate's own primitives — a runtime contract, an invariant, a gate, a receipt — so it
   can be tested with tooling that already exists (Umbrella for invariants, Beacon for
   signed evidence, Lantern for observability).
3. **Draft the request-for-artifacts.** Ask only for things that make an independent re-run
   possible: the exact literal text of the method; 3 representative tasks with model,
   provider, settings and dates; paired with/without runs; and 5–10 invariants written as
   yes/no observables. Never ask for their percentages — ask for material you can score
   yourself.
4. **Write the evaluation design before any data arrives.** Two baselines minimum (no
   method; a standard short governance header), the same tasks across models and
   operators, and pre-declared metrics: invariant violations per session, instruction
   adherence, output consistency, token usage, human rubric score.
5. **Receipt everything.** Results — positive, negative, or inconclusive — land as
   Beacon-signed receipts and are shared back with the claimant either way.
6. **Publish the exclusion.** If the Foundation evaluates a method, it states in the same
   breath which of the method's claims it did *not* test and does not endorse.

## Human gate  *(humans hold the meaning)*
A founder approves the reply before it is sent, and approves any publication of results.
Kindness and candor in the same letter is a human judgment, not an automated one.

## Never
- Never validate a claim by restating it in the Foundation's voice.
- Never let borrowed vocabulary ("quantum-inspired," "deterministic," "zero drift") into
  Foundation copy, even in quotation, without the exclusion stated beside it.
- Never respond with silence to a sincere contributor; a protocol *is* a respectful answer.
- Never accept a result you cannot re-run.
